## The Other Side of the Coin

Science typically excludes metaphysical claims from analysis. This paper inverts: **treat metaphysical hypotheses as falsifiable scientific claims** and apply rigorous evaluation. Not accepting or rejecting, but *modeling* — asking what would be true if these claims held, and what evidence would distinguish them.

---

## Abstract

This paper applies scientific methodology to frontier claims at the intersection of consciousness, physics, and information theory. It examines specific hypotheses — including those from channeled sources like Dolores Cannon — through the lens of computational epistemology, asking: what would falsify these claims? What would confirm them?

## The P = NP Consciousness Hypothesis

**Claim**: Human consciousness can solve NP-complete problems in polynomial time through "insight" or "intuition."

**Analysis**:

- Insight does not scale — humans cannot solve large SAT instances through intuition
- Apparent "sudden solutions" likely involve unconscious parallel search
- No evidence consciousness bypasses computational complexity bounds

**Verdict**: Unsupported by current evidence; reformulate as bounded speedup rather than complexity class collapse.

---

## The DNA Fractal Antenna Hypothesis

**Claim**: DNA's helical structure acts as a fractal antenna, receiving information from electromagnetic or non-physical fields.

**Analysis**:

- DNA does have RF absorption properties (physical fact)
- Fractal antenna theory applicable in principle
- No evidence of information reception beyond thermal noise
- Would require mechanism for information decoding

**Verdict**: Physically plausible mechanism, extraordinary claim requires extraordinary evidence, currently undemonstrated.
